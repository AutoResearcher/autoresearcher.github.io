Our Research

At AutoResearcher, we focus on developing autonomous systems that can accelerate scientific discovery across multiple domains. Our research combines machine learning, natural language processing, and automated experimentation techniques.

Autonomous Experimentation

We are developing systems that can design, execute, and analyze experiments with minimal human intervention. Our models can propose novel hypotheses, design appropriate experiments, and iteratively refine their understanding based on results.

  • Automated experimental design optimization
  • Hypothesis generation from literature
  • Closed-loop experimentation systems

Scientific Language Models

Our language models are specifically trained to understand, interpret, and generate scientific content. They can process research papers, extract meaningful insights, and identify connections between disparate fields.

  • Scientific literature comprehension
  • Cross-domain knowledge synthesis
  • Research question formulation

Multi-Modal Science Understanding

We're developing models that can understand scientific data across multiple modalities, including text, images, graphs, and structured data. This enables more comprehensive analysis and insight generation.

  • Scientific image analysis
  • Data visualization interpretation
  • Multi-modal research synthesis

Research Workflow Automation

Our systems aim to automate repetitive aspects of the research process, allowing scientists to focus on creative and high-level aspects of their work.

  • Automated literature reviews
  • Data preprocessing and analysis pipelines
  • Research report generation

Publications

α3: An Autonomous Experimental Design Framework for Scientific Discovery

Johnson, E., Chen, M., Rodriguez, S., et al.

Nature Machine Intelligence, 2025

We present α3, a framework for autonomous experimental design that can propose, refine, and execute scientific experiments across domains including materials science, chemistry, and biology.

FAST: A Tokenization Approach for Scientific Research Acceleration

Kim, D., Patel, J., Zhang, R., et al.

NeurIPS, 2024

We introduce FAST, a novel tokenization approach that improves the efficiency of training scientific language models by 5x compared to previous approaches.

α0: A Generalist Model for Scientific Discovery

Chen, M., Rodriguez, S., Johnson, E., et al.

Science, 2024

We describe the architecture and capabilities of α0, our first generalist model for scientific research, which demonstrates state-of-the-art performance across multiple scientific tasks.
